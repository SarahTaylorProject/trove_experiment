Early Warning Signs Model
supporting our students to succeed
Learn more about the new model developed 
to help identify the early warning signs of students who might be at risk and in need of support
Using predictive analytics, the Early Warning Signs (EWS) model pulls timely, in-depth data 
from over 70 sources, allowing the University to identify students who may be at risk 
Deputy Vice-Chancellor Education Professor Belinda Tynan said the Early Warning Signs model will 
continue to complement RMIT's existing student retention activities
that occur at a school, college and university-wide level. ​
The model provides in-depth data to further inform decision-making about our retention activities
both in the classroom and around the student experienceshe said. 
It also allows us to reach out to students that might be struggling 
and connect them to the amazing support services we offer
The Early Warning Signs model was first piloted last year (Semester 2)
and contributed to RMIT Connect and SLAMS Connect teams calling 1800 students offering support
Out of the 500 students who responded, 86 percent found the calls helpful 
and 37 percent put their hand up saying they needed help.
Data scientists Julie Dang and Theo Dufort
who both completed RMIT's Master of Analytics program
joined RMIT's Analytics and Insights Team in 2018 to develop the model.
As part of the pilot, 19 program teams were provided Early Warning Signs insights 
to complement their existing student retention activities
David McLay, Senior Data Manager, Learning Analytics Team 
said the model continues to be refined and tested with program teams.
In Semester 1 this year we met with 36 program teams and their support staff 
to review the data and workshop potential support strategies and actions  
We are scaling up the model this coming Semester 2 
with more programs, more data and more ideas about how we can improve the whole process
David says the future goal is to have a prescriptive model 
that not only identifies students at risk
but guides student retention activities.
In conjunction with the colleges, we’ve begun planning a 2020 rollout to all higher education programs
